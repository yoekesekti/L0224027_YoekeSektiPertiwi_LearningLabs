Welcome to Yogyakarta

Yogyakarta Special Region, abbreviated DIY, is a Special Region (a special province in Indonesia) which is a fusion of the Sultanate of Yogyakarta and the Duchy of Paku Alaman with the capital city of Yogyakarta. The Special Region of Yogyakarta is located in the southern part of Java Island, and borders Central Java Province and the Indian Ocean. This Special Region, which has an area of ​​3,185.80 km2, consists of one city and four districts. According to the 2010 population census, it has a population of 3,452,390 people with a proportion of 1,705,404 men and 1,746,986 women, and has a population density of 1,084 people per km2.

Vision of Special Region of Yogyakarta

Yogyakarta has become a city that excels in the fields of culture, tourism, and education, by blending tradition and sustainable innovation for the well-being of its people.

Mission of Special Region of Yogyakarta

  • Enhance the quality of life of the community through the development of the tourism and culture sectors.
  • Preserve and promote the unique local culture of Yogyakarta to the world.
  • Encourage innovation in the education sector to create competent human resources.
  • Become a world-class tourist destination that is friendly and safe for visitors.

Yogyakarta's Art & Culture

The city of Yogyakarta is one of the centers for preserving Javanese culture, especially the Yogyakarta style. Yogyakarta style Javanese culture has its own characteristics compared to Javanese cultural styles in other areas. This is due to the existence of the Yogyakarta Sultanate which chose to maintain the pure Javanese culture that had existed since the time of the Mataram Sultanate during the Jatisari Agreement.
